LEXIS — DAILY ADVANCED VOCABULARY FOR IOS
Product Requirements Document · v2.0
================================================================================

PROBLEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Most people want a stronger vocabulary but don't have a low-friction way to
build one. Flashcard apps are overwhelming. Word-of-the-day apps serve common
words. There's no tool that surfaces one genuinely rare, advanced word per day
— consistently and simply.


SOLUTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
An iOS app that uses the Claude API to surface one advanced, uncommon English
word each day — with its definition, etymology, pronunciation, and example
sentence. Users hear the word spoken aloud, review past words in an archive,
and are quizzed once a day at a time they choose.


TARGET USER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Curious, educated adults who read widely and want to expand their vocabulary —
writers, professionals, students preparing for GRE/GMAT, or anyone who enjoys
language.


SCREENS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Screen 1 — Today's word
  The word, phonetic pronunciation, part of speech, definition, etymology, and
  one example sentence. Fetched fresh each day from the Claude API. Cached
  locally so it loads instantly on revisit.
  [New] Tap-to-hear pronunciation via AVSpeechSynthesizer.

Screen 2 — Archive
  A scrollable list of all past words, sorted by date. Tap any word to see its
  full detail view. Stored locally with UserDefaults.

Screen 3 — Daily quiz
  Show today's word, present 4 definition choices (1 correct + 3 generated by
  Claude). User taps an answer, gets instant feedback. One attempt per day.
  [New] Locked until the user's scheduled quiz time.
  [New] Lock screen shows exactly when the quiz unlocks.

Screen 4 — Settings
  Two independent notification controls — one for the daily word, one for the
  quiz reminder. Each has an on/off toggle and a time picker. A notification
  preview shows exactly what will appear on the lock screen.
  [New] Quiz unlock time is driven by this setting.


LOOK & FEEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Theme
  Dark, quiet, literary. The app should feel like reading by lamplight — not
  like a productivity tool. Every design decision reinforces that this is a
  space for people who take language seriously.

Color
  Background is near-black with a warm undertone (#0f0e0d), never cold or
  neutral. Text is cream-toned (#f0ece4), not pure white, for easy reading.
  The only accent color is a muted gold (#c4a96b), used sparingly: streak
  dots, active tab indicators, and the pronunciation button. No other colors
  except a soft green (#6dba8f) for correct quiz answers.

Typography
  The word itself is always set in serif (Georgia). Everything else —
  labels, definitions, navigation — is sans-serif. This split creates a
  clear hierarchy: the word is the hero, everything else is support. Two
  weights only: regular and medium. Nothing heavier.

Spacing & surfaces
  Generous whitespace throughout. Cards and dividers use a very subtle
  border (0.5px, low opacity) so surfaces feel implied rather than boxed in.
  No gradients, no drop shadows, no decorative effects.

Interactions
  Restrained. A correct quiz answer glows green — it does not animate or
  celebrate loudly. The pronunciation button is a small gold play icon, not
  a prominent CTA. The lock screen is still and informational, not dramatic.
  The overall feeling is calm and considered, not gamified.

Navigation
  Four-tab bar at the bottom: Today, Archive, Quiz, Settings. Tabs are
  always visible. The active tab is indicated by a small gold dot above the
  label. The Quiz tab is always tappable — when locked it shows the lock
  screen rather than being disabled or hidden.


TECHNICAL DECISIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Word source       Claude API
  Framework         SwiftUI
  Pronunciation     AVSpeechSynthesizer
  Notifications     UserNotifications (local, no server)
  Local storage     UserDefaults
  Quiz gate         Time comparison via Date()


KEY PRODUCT RULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  01  One word per day. Never more, never skipped.

  02  Words must be genuinely advanced — not words most educated adults
      already know.

  03  The quiz is locked until the user's scheduled quiz time. Tapping early
      shows a lock screen with the unlock time — the tab is never hidden or
      disabled.

  04  One quiz attempt per day. Once answered, the result is shown but cannot
      be retried.

  05  Notifications are fully optional and independently configurable.


OUT OF SCOPE (V1)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - User accounts or sync across devices
  - Social sharing or streaks leaderboard
  - App Store submission
  - Human-recorded audio pronunciation


SUCCESS CRITERIA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
App runs on simulator, fetches a genuinely advanced word from Claude each day,
speaks it aloud on tap, stores history locally, locks the quiz until the
scheduled time, and delivers both notifications — all within a 1-hour build.
